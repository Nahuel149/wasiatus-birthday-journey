# Content Preparation Checklist

This checklist turns the idea into the material needed for development. Rough, personal writing is welcome; it can be refined later without losing Nahuel's voice.

## Essential facts

- [ ] Exact birthday date and intended reveal time
- [ ] Preferred spelling/capitalization of all names
- [ ] Years/dates for the major relationship milestones
- [ ] Safe display names for locations
- [x] Final decision: public GitHub Pages hosting with an unlisted shared link

## Core story chapters

For each chosen chapter, gather:

- [ ] Chapter title
- [ ] Approximate date range
- [ ] A 2–5 paragraph story in Nahuel's words
- [ ] The detail Wasiatus will instantly recognize
- [ ] One funny or tender moment
- [ ] One hero photograph
- [ ] Up to five supporting photographs
- [ ] Optional short video
- [ ] A transition or thought connecting it to the next chapter

Suggested chapters:

- [ ] How we met
- [ ] Our first conversation
- [ ] Our first date
- [ ] Funny and ordinary moments
- [ ] Trips together
- [ ] Wedding
- [ ] Pregnancy
- [ ] Rayden's birth
- [ ] Moving to Japan
- [ ] Family adventures
- [ ] Anniversaries and milestones
- [ ] Future dreams

## Love letter prompts

- What changed in your life when you met Wasiatus?
- What is something small she does that makes home feel like home?
- What do you admire about her as a partner?
- What do you admire about her as Rayden's mother?
- Which difficult moment showed the strength of your relationship?
- Which ordinary day would you happily live again?
- What do you hope the next ten years feel like?
- What promise do you want to end with?

## Reasons I Love You

Start with quality, not the number 100. Write 12–20 specific reasons for the MVP, then add more over time.

- [ ] Her personality
- [ ] The way she laughs or smiles
- [ ] How she cares for Rayden
- [ ] Things only Nahuel notices
- [ ] Shared jokes and phrases
- [ ] How she handles difficult days
- [ ] Her dreams and courage
- [ ] Everyday routines together
- [ ] Ways she has helped Nahuel grow
- [ ] Things about the future that feel exciting because she is there

Prefer “I love how you always…” plus a real example over broad phrases that could describe anyone.

## Photo selection

### First pass

- [ ] Copy candidates into a private working folder
- [ ] Remove obvious duplicates and blurred images
- [ ] Mark hero candidates
- [ ] Group by year/event
- [ ] Identify missing chapters

### For each selected photograph

- [ ] Stable ID/filename
- [ ] Title
- [ ] Date or honest approximate date
- [ ] People shown
- [ ] Category and tags
- [ ] Safe location label if relevant
- [ ] One-sentence description/alt text
- [ ] Optional story
- [ ] Favorite/featured status
- [ ] Focal point if faces are off-center
- [ ] Privacy/background review

## Video selection

- [ ] Keep only clips with a clear emotional role
- [ ] Trim dead time
- [ ] Choose/generate a poster frame
- [ ] Note date, people, place, and story
- [ ] Add captions/transcript when important speech is present
- [ ] Confirm the clip is appropriate for the chosen sharing model

## Music and sound

- [ ] Landing ambience choice, if any
- [ ] Love-letter piano choice, if any
- [ ] Birthday/finale music choice
- [ ] Song list with a short story for each track
- [ ] Publishing-rights check for locally hosted audio
- [ ] Written fallback for every external embed

## Finale

- [ ] Confirm final message
- [ ] Choose final hero image or family portrait
- [ ] Decide whether Rayden contributes a line, drawing, voice clip, or photo
- [ ] Decide what unlocks the ending
- [ ] Add a non-secret preview method for testing

## Easter egg ideas tied to real memories

- [ ] A hidden heart on the location of a first date
- [ ] A long-press revealing an outtake from a formal photograph
- [ ] A secret family joke
- [ ] A tiny Rayden drawing hidden in a chapter
- [ ] A recreated snippet of the first conversation

Choose a few strong surprises. Too many hidden targets can make a romantic journey feel like a scavenger hunt.

## Content readiness definition

Development can begin with placeholders, but the emotional MVP is content-ready when it has:

- 5–6 drafted chapters
- 10–15 complete memories
- 30–50 selected and described photographs
- 2–5 short selected videos
- A draft love letter
- 12–20 specific love reasons
- Final landing and finale copy
- A firm privacy/publishing decision
